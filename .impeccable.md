## Design Context

### Users
Personal website visitors — potential collaborators, investors, fellow founders, engineers, and people who come across Joshua's work. They're scanning quickly to understand who he is and what he's about. The site should reward that scan with clarity and substance.

### Brand Personality
Opinionated. Simple. Bright.

A product person's site — someone who cares about craft but doesn't need to prove it with visual tricks. The design should feel like a considered decision, not a template.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Visual tone:** Warm, clean, typographically driven. Let the type do the work.
- **References:** paco.me (sparse, structured, punchy), James Clear (warm, editorial, readable)
- **Anti-references:** Overly polished portfolios, dark mode dev-bro aesthetic, SaaS landing pages, LinkedIn-style flex copy, anything that smells like AI-generated marketing
- **Theme:** Light mode only
- **Signature accent:** Logo in bluestrap blue (`#3c9def`) — a nod to the original jkhowland.me blog

### Design Principles

1. **Typography is the design.** Serif/sans-serif pairing, intentional hierarchy, generous line height. No decoration needed when the type is right.
2. **Warm, not sterile.** Off-white backgrounds, earthy muted tones, gold-ish underlines. It should feel human and inviting, not clinical.
3. **Say less.** Short paragraphs, punchy lines, lots of whitespace. If it can be cut, cut it.
4. **No tricks.** No JavaScript unless absolutely necessary. No build step. No animations for the sake of animations. The content is the experience.
5. **Grounded copy.** Write like a person, not a brand. Avoid hollow claims, "not X but Y" patterns, and anything that reads like it was optimized for engagement.
